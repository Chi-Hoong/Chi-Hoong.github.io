Participant Information Sheet

General Outline of the Project

  • Description and methodology: Estimate the willingness-to-pay for a reduction in construction noise among residents in Singapore with a choice modelling survey.
  • Participants: We will be surveying 600 individuals through a laboratory-based survey from a panel of a survey company.
  • Use of data and feedback: The results of the survey will be published on ANU’s website and if you would like to receive updates on the outcomes of the research, you could leave your email with us at the end of the survey.
  • Project funding: This survey is funded by the Ministry of the Environment and Water Resources, Singapore.

Participant Involvement

  • Voluntary participation and withdrawal: Your participation in this research is voluntary, and you may decline to take part or to withdraw from the research without providing an explanation at any time until you have submitted the survey. As this survey is designed to be confidential you would not be able to withdraw your response, upon submission of the completed survey questionnaire.
  • What does participation in this survey entail?: You are invited to take part in a survey to ascertain the willingness-to-pay for a reduction in construction noise. You are invited to take part in a survey to ascertain the willingness-to-pay for a reduction in construction noise. The survey questionnaire consists of three main sections, (i) a hearing acuity test to understand your sensitivity to sounds; (ii) a series of questions where you choose between different types of construction noises and costs; and (iii) a demography section to understand your background.
  • Location and duration: The survey will be conducted in a laboratory at to be confirmed. The survey will consist of a single questionnaire which will require approximately 30 minutes to complete.
  • Remuneration: We will be providing an incentive of $10 worth of NTUC vouchers for participation in this survey to compensate for your time and travel costs to take this survey.
  • Risks: The research carries little risk, although you may feel uncomfortable when we are playing recorded construction noises to you, particularly if your hearing is sensitive or you have an existing hearing impairment. If you experience any discomfort during the survey, you can terminate the survey at any time. We would also appreciate your feedback should you experience any discomfort if you wish to inform us.
  • Benefits: In 2015, the National Environment Agency received more than 16,000 complaints related to construction noise, partly as a result of the high number of construction sites in Singapore. This project will provide us with an estimate of the benefits associated with an abatement of construction noise in Singapore, which would, in turn, enable us to propose policies to regulate construction noise. A reduction in noise levels will improve Singapore’s livability.

Exclusion Criteria

  • Participant limitation: You can only participate in this survey if you are a resident in Singapore and aged 18 or over.

Confidentiality

  • Confidentiality: We will keep your identity confidential as far as allowed by law, unless you elect to be named within the research. Access to the data you provide will be restricted to the research team, and identifying details will be stored separately from the rest of the research data. Published results will only be reported in aggregate, and you will not be identifiable within published outputs unless you have elected otherwise.

Privacy Notice

  • In collecting your personal information within this research, the ANU must comply with the Privacy Act 1988. The ANU Privacy Policy is available at https://policies.anu.edu.au/ppl/document/ANUP_010007 and it contains information about how a person can: (i) Access or seek correction to their personal information; and (ii) Complain about a breach of an Australian Privacy Principle by ANU, and how ANU will handle the complaint.

Data Storage

  • Where: Data will be securely stored on password protected computers at the Australian National University.
  • Duration: All research data will be retained and securely stored for at least five years following publications arising from research. Data will have no identifying details attached.
  • Handling of data following the required storage period: After the storage period, the data will be archived at the Crawford School of Public Policy for use in later research, including potentially by other researchers.
